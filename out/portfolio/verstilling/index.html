Project

Stillness

2025

A photographic series about stillness, choice, and withdrawal from the pace of the city.

A man bends into the soil. A body folds into the geometry of a staircase. Grass trembles in the wind. Dry thistles rise against a restless sky.

This series follows a quiet movement away from the speed and density of the city. Not as escape, but as a deliberate gesture: a choice for slowness, for attention, for another rhythm of living. The images move between human presence and landscape, between labour and stillness, between the body and the world that surrounds it.

Withdrawal here is not absence. It is a form of resistance - a quiet refusal of urgency. In the gestures of work, in the folding of a body, in the fragile persistence of plants against the sky, another tempo becomes visible.

These photographs inhabit that space: the fragile threshold between noise and silence.
